The Problem We're Solving

üö® A Silent Crisis in Nigeria

Over 589 million people globally live with diabetes, with 81% in low- and middle-income countries. In Nigeria alone, over 12 million adults are affected, making diabetes the leading cause of limb amputation after road traffic accidents.

The Current Reality:

  • 6.3% of people with diabetes undergo foot amputation annually (7.2% in Africa)
  • Diabetic foot ulcers account for 1 in 4 diabetes hospital admissions
  • 35.4% of DFU patients undergo amputation during hospitalization
  • 20.5% die during hospitalization

Why Current Methods Fail:

  • Annual foot exams are too infrequent to catch early changes
  • Pre-ulcerative inflammation goes undetected until it's too late
  • No affordable, accessible daily monitoring solution exists
  • By the time symptoms appear, significant damage has occurred

Diamon changes this. We catch the problem before it starts.
